Top 6 Benefits Of Living In Lagos

Ikeja • Lagos • Nigeria • Ikeja • Centre of Excellence.

Lagos, a metropolitan area in southwest Nigeria, is a bustling, vibrant, diverse, and diverse city. A hub for entertainment, culture, education, and international trade, Lagos has so much to offer anyone who ventures there! From historical and cultural landmarks to amazing shopping and commerce, Lagos is always sure to provide an amazing experience, whether you're visiting or just passing through!.

Here are the top 6 reasons for you to live in lagos.

1. Job Oppurtunities

It is of no doubt to anyone that Lagos provides more oppurtunities to pursue a career, from white collar jobs to lower jobs, it has it all. A recent article once encouraged Young African Enterpreneurs to reside in Lagos but this advice applies to literally anyone who is willing to make a mark. It is not only that there are more oppurtunities but higher pay and more benefits and financial compensation.

2. Night Life

Lagos is often described as the 'city that never sleeps', mostly because it doesn't sleep from parties, hangout spots, clubs and beer parlour, Lagos has it all. Excluding the fun, it doesn't sleep because you will find people who are still grinding late in the night or very early in the morning, which shows the dedication of "lagosians" which you can probably emulate.

3. Education

When it comes to education, Lagos is among the top, you have access to top quality schools for different budjets, so there are no worries finding good education for your children. The competition among other schools will only push yur child higher. A quick google search shows that there are over 5,000 schools in Lagos, so you have no worries about school as I have stated before.

4. Diversity in Background

When it comes to different background Lagos really has it all, you would see the like of Igbos, Yoruba, Hausas, Chinese, Indian, Togolese, Ghanian and other African countries. Socializing would be fun meeting all sorts of people with different backgrounds, beliefs and interests.

5. Recreational Centers

Lagos is the go to place for fun, it boasts of a large number of recreational and leisure centers ranging from theater, cinemas, beaches, malls, bars, night clubs to name a few, there are other places to have the time of your life either in the mainland or island, one thing is certain, it will be fun.
If you haven't been to Lagos, this should thrill you. If you do not believe, find someone from Lagos or has lived there and have them tell you the experience, If you do not come running to Lagos after this, I would be shocked.

6. Luxorious Lifestlyle

If you are the type that love that luxorious life, then Lagos is for you. The Lagos Island a part of Lagos state which is known for the luxurious features it offers residents. From five star hotels to five-star restaurants to the best art galleries, the most luxurious buildings to the best cars can be found in Lagos state. If you have the money and want that lifestyle, your daily life in Lagos will be luxurious.

Top 4 Places to Live In Lagos

All of this recommendation is highly subjective.

Ikoyi

Ikoyi is the best on the list, mainly because it balanced, there are no extremes or compromises. All aspects of an ideal environment, Ikoyi has it. With huge focus on security there is little to no crime, it has every basic amenity you could ever need. The ideal place for you to grow your family.

Yaba

Located in lagos mainland, it is largely considered as the Silicon Valley of Nigeria, majority of internet and tech startups have turned Yaba into this list.
Although it is no ikoyi, is still relatively balanced, from fun to housing to security. Yaba is ideal for you to grow your family on a lower budjet.

Ikeja

As the capital and business district of Nigeria, it houses a lot of government parastatals. It is higly industrial too with plenty of companies located there. With several Government Restricted Area, there are countless places for you to raise your family.

Magodo

One of the remote locations of the entire list, located around the outskirts of Lagos close to the likes of Ojodu Berger and Ikeja, Many affluent people have people seem to have taken advantage of this. This is also very balanced but it ranks lower because of accesibility but ideally this is among the greart places to raise your children.
